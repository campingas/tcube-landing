# Learning Science Résumé: 7 Steps to Learn Better
*Evidence-based methods — from memory science to purposeful motivation.*

---

### 1. Spacing & Compound Learning
**Build an early foundation.** Revisiting material at growing intervals exploits the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve, dramatically slowing down the rate of cognitive decay. Small daily gains compound over time into durable mastery.
* `Spaced repetition` `Ebbinghaus forgetting curve` `Compound learning` `Kumon method`

### 2. Active Recall & Engagement
**Move beyond passive re-reading.** Retrieval practice and elaborative interrogation rank among the highest-utility study techniques. Deep retention is achieved not by consuming information, but by producing it through teaching and doing.
* `Retrieval practice` `Elaborative interrogation` `Dunlosky (2013)` `Learning pyramid`

### 3. Test-Enhanced Learning
**Embrace the error notebook.** Utilizing the Chinese concept of *cuò tí běn* (錯題本) externalizes mistakes into primary study material. Introducing desirable difficulties—such as interleaving and testing over re-studying—produces far stronger long-term retention.
* `錯題本` `Test-enhanced learning` `Desirable difficulties` `Peer teaching`

### 4. Focused Attention
**Create a concentration bubble.** Deep work requires removing cognitive friction before it begins. Utilizing the Pomodoro technique, cardiac coherence breathing, or ambient noise reduces attentional residue, allowing the mind to enter a state of flow.
* `Flow state` `Pomodoro technique` `Cardiac coherence` `Single-goal focus`

### 5. Decision Fatigue Removal
**Shift execution to autopilot.** Making daily choices about what to study degrades willpower. Locking in a fixed weekly schedule removes the cognitive load of planning, preserving critical energy for the learning itself.
* `Decision fatigue` `Fixed weekly schedule` `Habit formation` `Cognitive load reduction`

### 6. Stress Inoculation
**Apply Pareto prioritization.** Building resilience requires gradual exposure to manageable challenges—productive struggle rather than avoidance. Applying the 80/20 rule focuses effort on the high-impact content that drives the majority of results.
* `Stress inoculation (SIT)` `Pareto 80/20` `Productive struggle` `Prioritisation`

### 7. Shimeikan (使命感)
**Cultivate a sense of mission.** The deepest engine of learning is intrinsic motivation. When a learner develops a personal calling that transcends grades or external rewards, all other learning techniques transition from friction into fuel.
* `使命感` `Intrinsic motivation` `Purpose-driven learning` `Long-term vision`
